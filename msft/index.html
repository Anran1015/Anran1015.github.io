Design Exercise

Topic 1: Car Parking in China

Car parking has always been an issue in most municipal cities in China. Despite efforts by officials from various government departments to put forth new policies and launch various solution proposals from time to time (followed by aggressive actions in the implementation of improvement action plans), their work seldom yields tangible outcomes, and the problem remains.

Today, we see more and more big cities in China, it becomes even worse and more common, parking challenges require creative solutions.

Please propose a creative solution to address this problem, it could be anything (App, Service, SW/HW) that works, feel free to make design assumptions and ideate based on them.

Research

Research plays a critical part in the design process. Considering the time I have for the design challenge, I did some quick desk research and interviews. To fully understand users’ needs and gain a deeper insight, methodologies such as shadowing and market analysis would also be beneficial.

Statistics & Facts

According to Beijing parking management report in 2017, at least 1.29 million spots are needed to close the gap between demands and car spots supply.

During night hours, the demands in residential parking is facing challenges (around 900k public parking spots are still available), while during the daytime, parking spot vacancy can be found in most of the residential communities.

Interviews

Key Questions Asked

  1. How’s your most recent parking experience?

  2. Have you ever parked randomly on the street? What’s the cause? What happened later on?

  3. What are some of the key factors you’d consider when it comes to parking spot searching? price/location/security etc.

  4. Do you own a parking spot in your community? Would you like to share it with other people when it is not occupied? Why or Why not?

  5. What’s the biggest challenge you are facing when parking off-street?

  6. Describe your ideal parking experience.

 
 

Ideation

After clustered ideas to identify pain points in the current parking experience. Based on my research, I decided to categorize the problems into two situations: parking spots demand in residential communities and public & commercial space (off-street parking). The key idea is to enable parking resource sharing during different period of a day.

 

Concept model

 

Design

The product has two core features: parking space sharing platform and real-time parking searching. The former is the foundation of the app allowing both party to connect and share parking resources, while the latter gives drivers access to real-time parking info to get off-street parking spots and shared parking spots instantly. Users could also book shared parking spots in advance on the platform.

Summary

This feature can be created as a standalone product, but can also be incorporated as a feature within GPS apps or transportation platforms. From a technical standpoint, leveraging existing data and technology solutions from platforms such as DiDi and Gaode map, the implementation would encounter less roadblocks and truly make impacts on the car parking issue. These platforms could also gain great benefits by embracing innovative solutions and take the lead in the market today.



Meanwhile, car parking issue also requires a shared effort from policy makers, urban design department and car owners. Utilizing emerging technologies, such as artificial intelligent, IOT, and latest automotive technologies, we could expect a brighter future in automotive and transportation solutions.
